The IP Value Chain
Eight stages from invention to monetisation
1
Invention & Ideation
The origin of IP. R&D activity generates novel ideas, processes, or technical solutions. Lab notebooks, invention disclosure forms (IDFs), and trade secret documentation begin here. Discovery must be recorded to establish priority dates.
Key actors
R&D teams, university labs, individual inventors
2
IP Identification & Capture
Screening inventions for patent eligibility — novelty, non-obviousness, industrial applicability. Deciding what to protect (patent, trade secret, copyright, design) and what to publish as defensive prior art to block competitors.
Key actors
IP counsel, CTOs, patent committees
3
Prior Art Search & Landscaping
Searching patent databases (USPTO, WIPO, EPO, CNIPA) to assess novelty, freedom-to-operate (FTO), and competitive landscape. Patent landscaping maps the existing IP terrain before investment in filing. AI tools are compressing this from weeks to hours.
Tools
PatSnap, Derwent, Google Patents, Questel
4
Patent Drafting & Filing
Attorneys draft claims — the legal core of the patent. Filing at a national office (USPTO, EPO, JPO, etc.) or via PCT (Patent Cooperation Treaty) for international protection across 150+ countries. Provisional applications allow priority date establishment with 12-month grace period.
Cost range
$5K–$25K per filing; $100K+ for global families
5
Prosecution & Grant
The back-and-forth between applicant and patent examiner — office actions, amendments, appeals. A patent is granted when the examiner accepts claims as valid. Duration: 2–4 years typical. Patents last 20 years from filing date, subject to maintenance fees.
Duration
2–4 years to grant; 20-year protection
6
Portfolio Management & Valuation
Active management of patent families — maintenance fee decisions, continuation filings, portfolio pruning. Valuation for M&A, licensing deals, balance sheet reporting, or securitisation. IP-backed lending and fractional IP finance are emerging asset classes.
Methods
Cost, market, income, and option-based approaches
7
Commercialisation & Licensing
Translating patent rights into revenue. Licensing (exclusive, non-exclusive, cross-licensing), joint ventures, technology transfer, and standard-essential patent (SEP) royalties. FRAND (Fair, Reasonable, Non-Discriminatory) licensing governs SEPs in telecoms, 5G, and Wi-Fi.
Revenue models
Royalties, lump-sum, cross-licence, spin-out
8
Enforcement & Litigation
Defending rights against infringement via cease-and-desist, injunctions, and litigation. Offensive assertion by Patent Assertion Entities (PAEs / patent trolls). Key venues: US ITC, USDC, EPO Opposition Division, and since 2023, the EU's Unified Patent Court (UPC). Inter Partes Review (IPR) is the fast-track patent challenge mechanism at the USPTO.
Typical cost
$2M–$10M+ per US district court litigation

Strategic insight: The value chain is not linear — companies loop back constantly. A new patent grant triggers fresh FTO searches; a litigation outcome reshapes filing strategy; licensing revenue funds the next R&D cycle. The most sophisticated patent programmes treat IP as a flywheel, not a filing checklist.

The intensity metric: Filing volume favours large economies. A better proxy for innovation density is patent applications per $100B GDP. Small Nordic nations consistently punch above their weight in per-capita innovation output.

2025–26 Trends
What's moving the patent landscape right now
  • ↑
    AI patents accelerating — up 33% since 2018

    AI-related applications appeared in 60% of all technology subclasses by 2023. Alphabet leads global AI patent filings (1,121 apps in 2024). Generative AI specifically is dominated by Alphabet, Zhejiang University, Microsoft, and Baidu. The USPTO now clarifies that only human inventors qualify — AI systems cannot be listed as inventors under Thaler v. Vidal — but humans using AI tools can still file.

  • ↑
    Clean tech and sustainability patents surging

    Green patent applications projected to spike 60% by 2025. Three of IFI's Top 10 fastest-growing technology categories in 2024 involve waste reduction — reclaiming non-ferrous metals (CAGR 26.2%), recovery of waste materials (26.1%), and destroying solid waste (17.7%). EV battery patents (CATL, LG Energy, Changxin Memory) growing fast.

  • ↑
    Medical and biotech patents surging post-COVID

    Medical-related patents saw a 76.3% increase in US grants in 2024 (30,429 to 53,648). Leading filers include Medtronic, Johnson & Johnson, Becton Dickinson, and Boston Scientific. CRISPR-Cas9 patent ownership (UC vs Broad Institute) remains one of the most consequential ongoing IP disputes in biotech.

  • →
    SEP / FRAND disputes intensifying in 5G era

    Standard-essential patents in 5G, Wi-Fi 6, and USB-C are generating significant licensing disputes. Qualcomm, Ericsson, Nokia, and Huawei hold major SEP portfolios. FRAND licensing terms are contested — who pays, how much, and under what terms remains litigated globally, including at the new EU Unified Patent Court.

  • ↑
    China's global share keeps rising — now 49% of all filings

    China went from 34.6% of global filings (2014) to 49.1% (2024). CNIPA nearly approached the 2-million-application mark. However, only 6.9% of Chinese filings are international — suggesting a primarily domestic defensive posture rather than a globalised portfolio. Huawei is the main exception, leading PCT filings globally for years.

  • ↑
    AI tools compressing the IP cycle dramatically

    AI is cutting patent valuation time by up to 60%. Semantic patent search (PatSeer, PatSnap Eureka, InnovationQ+) is replacing Boolean-only methods. AI-generated prior art disclosures are being used offensively to block future patentability — a novel tactic in IP strategy. Smart contracts are automating licensing terms on-chain.

  • →
    EU Unified Patent Court changes European enforcement

    The UPC, active since June 2023, creates a single patent litigation venue for participating EU member states. A single UPC judgment can revoke a patent across all participating states — dramatically raising the stakes for both patent holders and challengers. Early case volume suggests rapid adoption by pharma and tech companies.

  • ↑
    IP-backed finance and fractional IP emerging as asset class

    Patent portfolios are increasingly being used as collateral for secured lending. Fractional IP platforms and art-finance-style instruments are being explored for high-value patent families. This mirrors broader trends in alternative asset finance — treating IP as a balance sheet asset that generates both royalty income and debt-financing capacity.
